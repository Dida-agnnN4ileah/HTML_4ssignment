Information on some Disorders

1. Anxiety Disorders: These surround a range of conditions, including generalized anxiety
disorder(excessive worry about various aspects of life), social anxiety disorder,
and panic disorder (sudden, intense fear or discomfort accompanied by physical
symptoms like rapid heartbeat and sweating).

2. Depression: Often characterized by persistent feelings of sadness, hopelessness, or emptiness,
depression can also manifest as irritability, changes in appetite or sleep patterns,
and difficulty concentrating. Major depressive disorder is the most common form, but
other types include persistent depressive disorder and seasonal affective disorder.

3. Bipolar Disorder: Formerly known as manic depression, bipolar disorder involves
alternating periods of depressive episodes and manic or hypomanic episodes. During manic episodes,
individuals may experience heightened energy, euphoria, impulsivity, and decreased need for sleep,
while depressive episodes are marked by low mood, fatigue, and loss of interest or pleasure in activities.

4. Schizophrenia: This severe and chronic mental disorder affects how a person thinks,
feels, and behaves. Symptoms may include hallucinations, delusions, disorganized
thinking and speech, reduced emotional expression, and social withdrawal.

5. Obsessive-Compulsive Disorder (OCD): OCD involves intrusive, unwanted thoughts
that lead to repetitive behaviors or mental acts performed to reduce anxiety or prevent
a feared outcome. Examples include excessive hand washing, checking, counting,
or arranging objects in a specific way.

6. Post-Traumatic Stress Disorder (PTSD): PTSD can develop after experiencing or witnessing
a traumatic event such as combat, natural disaster, assault, or accident. Symptoms may include
intrusive memories or flashbacks, nightmares, avoidance of reminders of the trauma, negative
changes in mood and cognition, and increased excitement or reactivity.

7. Attention-Deficit/Hyperactivity Disorder (ADHD): ADHD is characterized by
persistent patterns of inattention, hyperactivity, and impulsivity that interfere with
daily functioning and development. Symptoms may include difficulty sustaining attention, excessive
fidgeting or squirming, and impulsively interrupting others.

8. Eating Disorders: These include conditions such as anorexia nervosa,bulimia nervosa,
and binge-eating disorder (frequent episodes of consuming large quantities of food
without control). Eating disorders often involve distorted body image and can have
serious physical health consequences.

9. Borderline Personality Disorder (BPD): BPD is characterized by unstable moods,
self-image, and relationships. Individuals with BPD may experience intense fear of abandonment,
emotional instability, impulsivity, and difficulty regulating emotions.They may also engage in self-destructive
behaviors or have a history of self-harm.

10. Substance Use Disorders: These involve the repetitive use of substances such as alcohol,
drugs, or medications despite negative consequences. Substance use disorders
can lead to physical dependence, tolerance, withdrawal symptoms, and impaired,
functioning in various areas of life.

11. PTSD in Children: Similar to PTSD in adults, children and adolescents may develop
PTSD after experiencing or witnessing a traumatic event. Symptoms may manifest
differently in children, including nightmares, bedwetting, separation anxiety,
regression in behavior, and somatic complaints.

12. Body Dysmorphic Disorder (BDD): BDD is characterized by obsessive preoccupation with
perceived flaws or defects in physical appearance, which are often
nonexistent or minor. Individuals with BDD may spend excessive time checking their
appearance in mirrors, seeking reassurance, or undergoing cosmetic
procedures to correct any 'flaws' that they think they have.
